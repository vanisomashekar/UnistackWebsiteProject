PRE-DEPARTURE CHECKLIST

Below, you will find a list of items and activities that you will need to take care of as you prepare to depart for your study abroad program. Please keep in mind that this list is not comprehensive and that your program will require action on your part above and beyond this list. Check your Pitt email for any important messages from your program manager.

  • VISA AND PASSPORT
  • Passport and visa in order.Make sure you have a valid passport. Your passport should not expire less than 90 days of your return to the U.S
  • Passport questionnaire complete. Always keep your passport in a document pouch/ money belt, along with other documents/money, when traveling.
  • Apply for a student visa, if necessary. Consult your host institution or program provider if you have questions specific to the visa requirements of your program.

  • FLIGHT BOOKING
  • Flight booked (with program manager approval)
  • Flight information questionnaire completed and uploaded to study abroad profile
  • Take a printout of flight ticket and keep it with you in the carry-on bag
  • Make sure you have sufficient time to settle down once you reach your destination. So book your tickets accordingly.

  • MEDICATIONS AND HEALTH INSURANCE
  • Visit to primary care physician and any other medical providers
  • Getting a physical before studying abroad is often overlooked, but it’s a necessary step in the pre-departure preparation. Ensure you are healthy and up-to-date on any necessary vaccines or shots before leaving.
  • Ask your doctor to write new prescriptions for any medication that’s about to expire, and get a recommendation on what to do if you’re sick or need to visit a hospital in your host country in case of a medical emergency.
  • Investigate options for comprehensive health insurance for your time abroad, and ask your local host institution or provider program if you will be covered by an in-country health insurance plan (and what is/is not covered by this plan). Be sure you understand the specific coverage and reimbursement policies of the health insurance plan(s) you elect.

  • BANKING AND CURRENCY
  • Find out what fees your bank and banks abroad charge for transactions and plan to take several forms of funds with you (i.e. credit cards, travelers checks, cash, etc).
  • Consult currency conversion websites for currency exchange information before and while traveling: http://www.xe.com/ucc

  • MAKE MULTIPLE PACKING LISTS
  • Packing can be an overwhelming process, especially when it’s tempting to throw everything you own into your suitcases. Remember to take along adapter plugs, a language translation device or dictionary, snacks from home and a list of emergency contacts.
  • Find out what’s affordable and easy to find in your host country from hair products to contact lens solution. You may discover it’s easier and more affordable to just purchase necessary items while you’re there. Otherwise, make a list of all the personal care products you need and remember to pack them away in a checked bag to avoid issues at airport security.
  • Check the weather in your new country a few months in advance to figure out how to maximize your wardrobe and layer together outfits that can work whether it’s chilly or warm. Ask your parent or a roommate if they can ship you pre-marked boxes in three or four months and receive packages back from you.

  • IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS TO CARRY
  • Marksheets of X, XII and undergraduation.
  • Transcripts and Degree certificate.
  • SOPs and LORs
  • Financial documents
  • VISA and I-20
  • Identity cards other than passport
  • Extra copies of all the above mentioned documents
